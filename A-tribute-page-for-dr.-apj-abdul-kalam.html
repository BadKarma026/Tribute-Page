Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India | People's President

Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam

Born: 15 October 1931

Place of Birth: Rameswaram, Madras Presidency, British India (present-day Tamil Nadu, India)

Died: 27 July 2015 (aged 83) Shillong, Meghalaya, India

Here's a time line of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931: Born on 15 October 1931, in Rameswaram on Pamban Island (now in Tamil Nadu).
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from the Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, then affiliated with the University of Madras.
  • 1955: Moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960: Graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology and joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (by Press Information Bureau, Government of India) as a scientist after becoming a member of the Defence Research & Development Service (DRDS).
  • 1969: Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1970 - 1990: Made an effort to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects, both of which proved to be successful.
  • 1970: Directed two projects, Project Devil and Project Valiant, which sought to develop ballistic missiles from the technology of the successful SLV programme.
  • 1980: Became the project Director for India's First Indegenous Satellite Program.
  • 1992: Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation from July 1992 to December 1999.
  • 1997: Awarded Bharat Ratna, the highest civilian award of the Republic of India.
  • 2002: Won the presidential elections, succeeding K.R. Narayanan and becoming the 11th president of India.
  • 2007: His term as the President of India. He also didn't partake in the elections for a second term stating that he wanted to avoid involving Rashtrapati Bhavan from any political processes.
  • 2012: Launched a programme for the youth of India called the What Can I Give Movement, with a central theme of defeating corruption.
  • 2015: Died at the age of 83.
